Dashboard Guide

Introduction

Your Linkyoh dashboard (also known as "My Gigs") is your control center for managing all your service listings. It provides a comprehensive overview of your gigs, their performance, and tools to manage them effectively.

With your dashboard, you can:

  • View all your active and inactive gigs in one place
  • Track performance metrics like views and likes
  • Edit, activate, or deactivate gigs
  • Create new gigs
  • Monitor customer engagement

This guide will help you navigate your dashboard and make the most of its features to effectively manage your service listings on Linkyoh.

Accessing Your Dashboard

There are several ways to access your dashboard on Linkyoh:

1From the Navigation Menu
  1. Click on your profile picture or username in the top-right corner of any page
  2. A dropdown menu will appear
  3. Click on "My Gigs"
2Direct URL

You can also access your dashboard directly by going to:

https://linkyoh.com/my-gigs/
Pro Tip

Bookmark your dashboard page for quick access. This is especially useful if you manage multiple gigs and need to check their performance regularly.

Dashboard Overview

Your dashboard is designed to give you a clear overview of all your gigs and their performance. Here's what you'll find on your dashboard:

Gig Listings

A list of all your gigs, both active and inactive

Performance Metrics

View counts, likes, and other engagement metrics

Management Tools

Options to edit, activate, or deactivate your gigs

Create New Gig

Quick access to create a new service listing

1Dashboard Layout

Your dashboard is organized to make it easy to find and manage your gigs:

Dashboard Components:
  • Header Section: Shows your total number of gigs and a button to create a new gig
  • Gig Cards: Each of your gigs is displayed as a card with key information and action buttons
  • Metrics Display: Each gig card shows performance metrics like views and likes
  • Action Buttons: Options to view, edit, or change the status of each gig

Managing Your Gigs

Your dashboard provides several tools to help you manage your gigs effectively. Here's how to use them:

1Creating a New Gig
  1. From your dashboard, click the "Create New Gig" button
  2. Fill out the gig creation form with all required information
  3. Click "Publish Gig" to make your gig live

For detailed guidance on creating gigs, see our How to Add a Gig guide.

2Editing an Existing Gig
  1. Find the gig you want to edit on your dashboard
  2. Click the "Edit" button (pencil icon) on the gig card
  3. Make your changes in the edit form
  4. Click "Save Changes" to update your gig
Pro Tip

Regularly updating your gigs with fresh information, new photos, or updated pricing can help keep them relevant and attractive to potential customers.

3Viewing Your Gig
  1. Find the gig you want to view on your dashboard
  2. Click the "View" button (eye icon) on the gig card
  3. This will take you to the public view of your gig, exactly as customers see it
4Changing Gig Status
  1. Find the gig you want to update on your dashboard
  2. Click the "Active/Inactive" toggle switch on the gig card
  3. Confirm your choice when prompted
Important

When you deactivate a gig, it will no longer appear in search results or category listings. However, the URL will still work if someone has it bookmarked or saved. To completely remove a gig, you need to delete it.

Performance Metrics

Your dashboard displays key performance metrics for each of your gigs, helping you understand how they're performing and which ones are attracting the most interest.

1Understanding Metrics
Metric Description Why It Matters
Views The number of times your gig has been viewed Indicates how many people are seeing your gig
Likes The number of users who have liked your gig Shows interest level and potential customer engagement
Last Updated When you last made changes to your gig Helps you keep track of when information might need refreshing
Status Whether your gig is active or inactive Indicates if your gig is visible to potential customers
2Detailed Analytics

For more detailed analytics about your gig's performance:

  1. Click on the "Stats" or "Analytics" link on a gig card (if available)
  2. View detailed information about views over time, user engagement, and more

For more information on understanding and using metrics, see our Metrics Guide.

Pro Tip

Compare metrics across your gigs to identify what works best. If one gig is getting significantly more views or likes, consider what makes it more appealing and apply those elements to your other gigs.

Gig Status

Managing the status of your gigs is an important part of maintaining your presence on Linkyoh. You can control whether each gig is visible to potential customers.

1Status Types
Active

When a gig is active:

  • It appears in search results
  • It's visible in category listings
  • Users can find and interact with it
  • It can receive views, likes, and inquiries
Inactive

When a gig is inactive:

  • It doesn't appear in search results
  • It's hidden from category listings
  • The URL still works if someone has it saved
  • It can still be viewed, but won't attract new customers
2When to Change Status
Scenario Recommended Status Why
You're currently available for work Active Makes your services visible to potential customers
You're temporarily unavailable (vacation, fully booked, etc.) Inactive Prevents inquiries when you can't take on new work
You're updating your gig information Inactive (temporarily) Prevents customers from seeing incomplete information
You no longer offer this service Inactive or Delete Removes services you no longer provide
Pro Tip

Instead of deactivating a gig when you're temporarily unavailable, consider updating the description to indicate your availability timeline. This keeps your gig visible and lets potential customers know when you'll be available again.

Dashboard Tips

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your dashboard and effectively manage your gigs:

Regular Updates

Update your gigs regularly with fresh information, new photos, or seasonal offerings to keep them relevant and attractive.

Monitor Performance

Check your metrics regularly to understand which gigs are performing well and which might need improvements.

Diversify Your Offerings

Create multiple gigs for different services you offer to reach a wider audience and increase your visibility.

Preview Your Gigs

Regularly view your gigs as customers see them to ensure they look professional and contain accurate information.

Seasonal Updates

Update your gigs for holidays or seasonal changes to stay relevant and capture seasonal demand.

Remove Outdated Gigs

Delete or deactivate gigs for services you no longer offer to keep your profile current and focused.

Frequently Asked Questions

You can create as many gigs as you want on Linkyoh. However, we recommend focusing on quality over quantity. It's better to have a few well-crafted, detailed gigs than many incomplete or low-quality ones.

Deactivating a gig:

  • Hides it from search results and category listings
  • Keeps the gig in your dashboard
  • Allows you to reactivate it later
  • Preserves all gig data, metrics, and history

Deleting a gig:

  • Permanently removes the gig from Linkyoh
  • Cannot be undone
  • Removes all associated data and metrics
  • Frees up the title and content for use in new gigs

Metrics like views and likes can fluctuate for several reasons:
  • System updates or data corrections
  • Removal of spam or bot activity
  • Users unliking a previously liked gig
  • Temporary reporting delays or glitches
If you notice a significant unexplained decrease, please contact our support team.

Currently, gigs are displayed in order of creation date, with the newest gigs appearing first. We're working on adding sorting and filtering options to the dashboard in future updates to make it easier to manage multiple gigs.

To increase views and engagement:
  • Use clear, descriptive titles with relevant keywords
  • Add high-quality images that showcase your services
  • Provide detailed descriptions of what you offer
  • Keep your gig information up-to-date
  • Add multiple service areas to reach more customers
  • Share your gig links on social media or with your network
  • Respond promptly to inquiries to build a good reputation
